2Three evidence streams
The system

Three independent streams of evidence.

STREAM 01

Daily sessions

Every lesson logged by the coach who taught it: letters and skills covered, group composition, attendance, duration.

Answers: are children receiving instruction — the right instruction, at the right pace?
STREAM 02

Formal assessments

One-minute EGRA letter fluency plus reading, writing and nine numeracy skills — at baseline, midline and endline, child by child.

Answers: are children actually learning?
STREAM 03

Mentor visits

Trained mentors observe sessions in classrooms, score implementation quality, coach the youth, and log action items.

Answers: why are the numbers what they are — and what do we do next?
3Triangulation
Why three streams

Each stream checks the other two.

High session counts with low learning gains? Mentor observations tell us whether the problem is pace, group composition, or fidelity. Strong assessment results? Session logs confirm the dosage that produced them.

And when two fully independent measurement methods agree, we trust both more.

What we measure

Every skill that makes a reader — and a counter.

Improvement reported four ways: raw points · standardised (0–10) · percent change · learning gain %. The one-minute letter task is the anchor — it is the strongest single predictor of future reading.

Technical note for due-diligence readers
2026 assessments sync from the TeamPact API via a two-step resolution: survey responses provide a group ID, which is resolved to class and programme; grade derives from class name, with learner-baseline fallback for letter-group labels. ECD baseline 2026 lacked participant IDs, so the ECD midline comparison is cross-sectional rather than matched-learner — and is labelled as such wherever it appears. Historical 2023/24 data is served from Parquet archives. Full data-source documentation is available in the due-diligence pack.
8Quality & privacy

We flag our own problems first.

Automated quality checks run across implementation data, flagging patterns that may reduce learning quality before they show up in results.

Example flag: a coach advancing groups to new letters with no review built in — caught by the "moving too fast" check, resolved through a mentor visit, not a report at year end.

Children's privacy is a design constraint.

Public dashboards show aggregate, anonymized results only. Names and sensitive fields are masked in every unauthenticated view; detailed records live behind authenticated, role-based access for programme teams.

In practice: the child profile on our impact page uses a changed name and a guardian-consented photo — POPIA compliance is checked before anything is published.
